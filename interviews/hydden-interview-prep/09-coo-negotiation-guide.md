## Compensation Strategy

### Should You Stick to 75 LPA? **YES, but with flexibility**

**Why 75 LPA is Reasonable:**

1. **15% increase over total comp** (65L → 75L)
   - Industry standard for role change: 15-30%
   - You're at lower end, justified by startup excitement

2. **Risk premium for startup**
   - Leaving stable Couchbase for earlier-stage Hydden
   - Less certainty around funding, market fit
   - 15% compensates for increased risk

3. **Market rates for Senior Full Stack (10+ years)**
   - Bangalore/Remote: 60-90 LPA range
   - 75 LPA is mid-range (defensible)

4. **You're bringing proven value**
   - Production systems at scale (SSE architecture)
   - Relevant domain (RBAC, distributed systems)
   - Can contribute immediately

### Compensation Structure to Discuss

**Don't just focus on "75 LPA base" - negotiate the package:**

```
Total Compensation = Base + Equity + Bonus + Benefits

Option A: Higher Base, Lower Equity
- Base: 75 LPA
- Equity: 0.05-0.10% (industry standard for senior engineer at Series A/B)
- Bonus: 10-15% performance bonus
- Total: ~82-86 LPA (Year 1)

Option B: Balanced
- Base: 70 LPA
- Equity: 0.15-0.20% (4-year vest)
- Bonus: 10-15%
- Total: ~77-80 LPA (Year 1, more if equity appreciates)

Option C: Startup Bet
- Base: 65 LPA (match current)
- Equity: 0.25-0.30% (larger stake)
- Bonus: 15-20%
- Total: ~75-78 LPA (Year 1, significant upside if Hydden succeeds)
```

**My Recommendation: Start with Option A (75 LPA base)**

Why:
- Establishes floor (you're not taking a pay cut)
- Still get equity upside
- Less risky than Option C
- Can negotiate down to Option B if needed

### Understanding Equity Value

**Ask these questions:**

1. **"What's the current valuation?"**
   - If Series A: $10-50M valuation
   - If Series B: $50-200M valuation
   
2. **"How many shares outstanding?"**
   - Your % × Total shares = Your shares
   
3. **"What's the vesting schedule?"**
   - Standard: 4 years, 1-year cliff, monthly vesting
   - Aggressive: 3 years (faster)

4. **"What's the strike price for options?"** (if options, not RSUs)
   - Lower is better
   
5. **"Any acceleration clauses?"** (double-trigger)
   - If Hydden acquired, do you vest immediately?

**Example Calculation:**

```
Scenario:
- 0.15% equity
- Current valuation: $50M
- 4-year vest

Your equity value TODAY: 0.15% × $50M = $75,000 over 4 years
= ~$18,750/year (if sold today, which you can't)

If Hydden exits at $500M in 5 years:
Your equity: 0.15% × $500M = $750,000
Diluted to ~0.10% (funding rounds): $500,000
Still significant upside!

If Hydden fails: $0

This is the risk/reward.
```

## Roles & Responsibilities Discussion

### What to Clarify

**1. Role Scope**

**Ask:**
> "Can you walk me through what my first 90 days would look like? What would be my primary focus area?"

**Listen for:**
- ✅ Specific projects (connector development, data pipeline, frontend)
- ✅ Clear ownership (full-stack = both backend & frontend?)
- ⚠️ Vague ("help the team", "we'll figure it out") = red flag

**2. Team Structure**

**Ask:**
> "What does the engineering team look like? Who would I be working with most closely?"

**Listen for:**
- Team size (5 engineers? 20?)
- Reporting structure (report to CTO? Engineering Manager?)
- Frontend/Backend split
- AI team interaction

**3. Tech Stack & Autonomy**

**Ask:**
> "I saw the JD mentions Java, Node.js, Go, Python. What's the current stack, and what flexibility do I have in choosing technologies for new projects?"

**Listen for:**
- ✅ "Mostly Go for backend, you'd own the connectors" = clear
- ✅ "You can propose and justify" = autonomy
- ⚠️ "We use all of them" = potentially messy codebase

**4. Growth & Career Path**

**Ask:**
> "What does career growth look like at Hydden? Is there a path to Staff/Principal engineer or engineering leadership?"

**Listen for:**
- ✅ Clear leveling framework
- ✅ Promotion criteria
- ⚠️ "Too early to think about that" = might be true, but note it

**5. On-Call & Work-Life Balance**

**Ask:**
> "What does the on-call rotation look like for a security-critical product? How does the team handle work-life balance?"

**Listen for:**
- On-call frequency (1 week/month? 1 week/quarter?)
- Escalation process
- Compensation for on-call
- Actual working hours culture

**6. Remote Work Policy**

**Ask:**
> "I saw this is a remote role. What's the expectation for in-person meetings? Any required office days?"

**Listen for:**
- ✅ Fully remote (team distributed)
- ⚠️ "We expect you in office 2-3 days/week" (conflicts with WFH in JD)

## Negotiation Tactics

### The Power of Silence

After they make an offer:

```
COO: "We can offer 65 LPA base + 0.1% equity."

You: [Pause 5-10 seconds. Look thoughtful. Don't rush to respond.]

You: "I appreciate that. Let me make sure I understand..."
```

**Why it works:** Silence makes them uncomfortable, they might improve offer

---

### The Anchoring Effect

**Your anchoring strategy:**

```
Them: "What are your salary expectations?"

You: "Based on my research and current compensation, I'm looking at 75 LPA base as a starting point. But I'm open to discussing the complete package including equity and bonuses. What's the range for this role?"
```

**Why 75L anchor:**
- Sets high expectation
- "Starting point" = room to negotiate down
- Final offer likely lands at 70-75L

---

### The Justification Triple

When negotiating, use this pattern:

```
I'm looking for [X] because:

1. [Market data]: "Senior full-stack engineers with 10+ years in Bangalore are at 60-90L range"

2. [Your value]: "I bring production experience with distributed systems, RBAC, and can contribute immediately to your connector architecture"

3. [Risk premium]: "I'm leaving a stable position at Couchbase for an early-stage startup, which carries inherent risk"
```

**Why it works:** Logical, data-driven, hard to refute

---

### The Alternative Option

Always give them a choice:

```
"I understand if 75L base is a stretch. Here are two alternatives:

Option A: 70L base + 0.2% equity
Option B: 72L base + 0.15% equity + 15% performance bonus

Which is more feasible?"
```

**Why it works:** 
- Collaborative, not adversarial
- They feel in control
- You still get closer to target

---

### The Enthusiasm Balance

**Throughout negotiation, maintain:**

```
✅ "I'm really excited about Hydden's mission and the technical challenges"
[Shows: You want to join]

✅ "I want to make sure the compensation reflects the value I'm bringing and the risk of moving from an established company"
[Shows: You're thoughtful, not desperate]

❌ "I need 75L or I'm not joining"
[Shows: Ultimatum, adversarial]

❌ "Whatever you offer is fine"
[Shows: Desperate, undervalues yourself]
```

## Special Considerations

### 1. Signing Bonus

**If they can't meet your base salary:**

> "I understand 75L base might be challenging. Would a signing bonus be possible to bridge the gap? For example, 70L base + 5L signing bonus gets us to my target for Year 1."

**Why it works:**
- One-time cost (easier for company)
- You still get your money
- Base matters more long-term (raises are % of base)

---

### 2. Performance Bonus

**Ask:**

> "Is there a performance bonus component? What's the target and stretch?"

**Typical structure:**
- Target: 10-15% of base (if you meet goals)
- Stretch: 15-20% of base (if you exceed)

**Negotiate:**
> "Could we structure this as 70L base + 15% target bonus? That gets me to ~80L total comp if I perform well, which I'm confident I will."

---

### 3. Annual Raises

**Ask:**

> "What's the typical annual raise cycle? Is it performance-based or company-wide?"

**Why it matters:** 
- If no raises planned: Your 70L base stays 70L for years
- If 8-10% annual: You'll catch up to market quickly

---

### 4. Equity Refresh

**Ask:**

> "After the initial equity vests, is there a refresh program to retain senior engineers?"

**Why it matters:**
- After 4 years, your equity is gone
- Good companies give new grants annually

## Red Flags to Watch For

### In Compensation Discussion

🚩 **"We don't negotiate"**
- Rigid companies struggle with senior talent

🚩 **"Trust us, the equity will be worth millions"**
- Maybe, but need base salary to live on

🚩 **Vague on equity details**
- "We'll figure it out later" = not transparent

🚩 **"Everyone is paid the same"**
- Ignores market rates and experience

### In Role Discussion

🚩 **"You'll wear many hats"**
- Could mean: no clear role, do everything

🚩 **"We move fast and break things"**
- Could mean: no process, chaotic

🚩 **Can't articulate your first project**
- They don't know what they need

🚩 **"We're like a family"**
- Often means: long hours, guilt-tripping

### In Culture Discussion

🚩 **Work-life balance question met with discomfort**
- Expect long hours

🚩 **Can't name recent failures or what they learned**
- Blame culture

🚩 **Only talks about raises/promotions as "maybe in future"**
- No career growth path
